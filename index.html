By many regarded as the first programmer

Below is a time line of Ada Lovelace's life:

  • 1815 - Born as the only legitimate child of the poet known as Lord Byron
  • 1816 - Ada's parents separate and Ada moves with her mother to her grandparents' home
  • 1828 - Decides, at the age of 12, that she wants to fly and starts constructing wings by studying birds and their proportions. She even illustrated a guide called “Flyology”
  • 1833 - She meets the mathematician, philosopher and inventor Charles Babbage. They become friends and throught her life they had a correspondence concerning Babbage's prototypes "The difference engine" and "The analytical machine".
  • 1842–43 - She translates an article on Babbage's Analytical Engine including her own set of notes, which were three times as long as the original article, on the subject. In her notes she describes among other things a method for calculating a sequence of Bernoulli numbers with the Engine. This method is widely considered to be the first computer program and her work marks her as the first computer programmer.
  • 1844 - Starts working on several projects. These include:
    • creating a mathematical model for how the brain gives rise to thoughts and to feelings.
    • writing a review of a paper by Baron Karl von Reichenbach, "Researches on Magnetism".
    Neither of these works however were published.
  • 1851 - Gets uterine cancer while allegedly working on productions regarding the relation of maths and music.
  • 1852 - Dies at the age of 36.
